Maria Skłodowska Curie
Pure genius

Marie Curie is considered to be one of the most influential women, whose achievements flipped aout world upsite down. Her discoveries made it possible, among other things, to cure skin cancer and to perform X-rays.

Childhood

Maria Skłodowska Curie was born in 1867 in Warsaw. She spent her childhood and youth in this city. From an early age, she was very talented. She quickly learned how to read and write. She grew up in a family of teachers. Her father Władysław was a physicist and mathematician, so he used every free moment to teach young Skłodowska's family something. When Marysia was 11 years old, her mother died. At that moment she lost her faith in God and fell into depression. She started her education at the girls' boarding school at the age of 10. She was then educated at the 3rd Female Government Gymnasium, from which she graduated with outstanding results and a gold medal. Skłodowska Curie and her two sisters entered the Flying University when they were only 16 years old. During this time they met eminent professors and significantly increased their knowledge.

Studies

At the beginning of 1891, Maria decided to go to Paris to begin studying at a French university in November. She chose mathematics and physics as subjects for her studies. Interestingly, she also devoted her free time to amateur theatre acting. After completing her studies in 1893, thanks to Lippmann she received a scientific scholarship for her research on the magnetic properties of steel. Shortly afterwards, she met Peter Curie, who was 8 years older and with whom she married on 26 July 1895. Immediately after their wedding they went on their honeymoon on bicycles which they had received from a friend.

Research career

At the end of 1897, Marie begins her scientific research with Becquerel's rays in order to obtain her PhD. At that time, she drew valid conclusions, made many scientific hypotheses and proved most of them. Her husband joined in the research. Using chemical transformations, they isolated a new element (Polonium) - named after Poland. Shortly afterwards, in 1898, they reported the discovery of another element - radium. For their achievements, the couple were awarded numerous prizes including the Planie, Lacaze, Nobel. After Pierre's death, Marie devoted herself entirely to her work and in 1911 she was awarded the Nobel Prize for Chemistry on her own, thus becoming the first person to receive this prize twice.

End of life

Marie convinced the French government to build the Radium Institutes. This is what happened. In 2014, the building was erected. Later, four more Nobel laureates came out of it. After the outbreak of World War I, Sklodowska Curie saved many people with the help of X-ray machines. In 1916, she became one of the first women to obtain a driver's license. Five years later, Maria and her daughters traveled to the United States. In 20s, she began to have health problems. She was diagnosed with aplastic anemia. Maria Skłodowska Curie died in a sanatorium on July 4, 1934.

I Nobel's prize 1903

The Curie couple, together with Henri Becquerel, receive the Nobel Prize for their research into radioactivity. They proved that uranium (and some chemical elements) emitted new kind of radiation.

II Nobel's prize 1911

On 9 November 1911 the Swedish Academy of Sciences awarded Maria a second, this time independent, Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the discovery of polonium and radium. She became the first person to be awarded this prize twice and the first woman Nobel Prize winner in chemistry.

Construction of the radium institute 1914

Maria Skłodowska Curie built a radium institute with the help of the French government. Research was conducted there in several fields, including physics, chemistry and medicine. It was attended by 4 Nobel Prize winners, which is why it was called the forge of Nobel Prize winners.

Burial in the Paris pantheon 1955

In 1955 Marie Curie and her husband are buried in the Panthéon in Paris. Interestingly, Marie Curie Sklodowska is the first woman and the first non-French person to be buried there.

White eagle order 2018

Not so long ago, because in 2018, maria skłodowska curie and her husband were posthumously honoured with the Order of the White Eagle, which is the highest reward of the Republic of Poland.

Fact #1

She was an extremely talented, able to read fluently at the age of four. She also began learning foreign languages at a young age, eventually becoming fluent in as many as five languages.

Fact #2

Her image can be seen on many banknotes and coins. In Poland it appeared on 2 banknotes and 6 coins, in France on 1 banknote and 1 coin, and in Spain on one coin.

Fact #3

Maria Skłodowska had two daughters. One of them, Irena, followed in her footsteps and in 1935 received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry. Her second daughter Eve, on the other hand, was a politician and writer.

Fact #4

Marisa Skłodowska Curie, knew many of the most famous scientists, including Ernest Solvay and even Albert Einstein, with whom she corresponded for a long time. It is believed that Alberto Einstein owed his scientific career to Maria.
