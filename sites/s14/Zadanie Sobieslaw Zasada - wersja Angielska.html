Introduction

Sobieslaw Zasada is one of the best Polish rally drivers. During his long career, he won practically everything he could. Unfortunately, the time of his career falls on a period when communism reigned in Poland. The authorities, to put it mildly, did not make his career easy, the need for frequent changes of pilots and problems with obtaining visas to foreign countries were not the only problems he encountered on his way. Despite this he managed to become a factory Porsche driver and became the best rally driver not only behind the Iron Curtain but in the whole world. In his break from racing, he wrote books that constantly inspire young people to pursue their goals despite adversities. Probably everyone knows the saying "Have a nice road", popular in Poland, but hardly anyone knows that it was invented by him.

Biography under 37

Both these things had a great impact on his further career; sport gave him fitness and the ability to constantly work on his personal development, while scouting taught him self-discipline. He won his first rally in February 1952, the right seat was occupied by Ewa Goworowska, who became his wife the same year. Ewa always supported her husband, she was often his pilot, even on the most difficult rally routes. In 1953, a terrible accident happened, which could end the champion's career before it began for good. While skiing in Zakopane, he suffered an open leg fracture, doctors miraculously saved it from amputation. This unpleasant event ended the athletic career of Zasada, from that moment on he focused on rallying. Sobies≈Çaw started with his private cars such as BMW 328 or Fiat 600d. At the Monte Carlo Rally in 1964, he was spotted by the factory team manager of Styer-Puch, who told him to come to the factory after the rally, and he would get a rally car there. Thus began a new phase in the champion's career. For more than three years he was a factory driver for this company. Driving a small, light, but unfortunately weak car, he not once defeated his rivals in faster and more powerful cars. In 1966, he managed to win the title of European Rally Champion with this car, which in those days meant becoming a World Rally Champion! To this day, Mr. Zasada fondly remembers those times, when everyone was amazed at what could be done with such a small Styer-Puch.

Biography over 37

Sobieslaw knew Ferdinand Porsche personally, and this acquaintance led to his appearance in a Porsche 911 in the 1967 Gran Premio de Argentina. The entire board of directors of the company agreed that their car was not capable of completing the entire route of this difficult rally. The route was over 4000 kilometers long, and most of it was on roads that were in a terrible condition. Nevertheless, after long and exhausting negotiations he managed to get a rally car. However, he did not start as a factory team, but as a private driver with Porsche service. This procedure allowed the factory to disavow a seemingly certain defeat. However, to the surprise of the entire world, Mr. Zasada won that rally and officially introduced Porsche to rallying. He returned to Europe as a king, at the airport he was greeted pompously by the entire management of the company, headed by Mr. Porsche himself. That year as a Porsche factory driver he won his second European Rally Championship title. His adventure with Porsche lasted another 5 years. During this period he won the title of European Rally Champion 2 times, European Vice-Champion 3 times and Polish Rally Champion 3 times. The end of such an outstanding career had to take place during some special event. The choice fell on the Vuelta del America del Sur rally. The route led around South America and amounted to 30 thousand kilometers. Mr Sobies≈Çaw driving a Mercedes finished the rally in a phenomenal 2nd place, it was in 1978. In 1997, at the age of 67, he and his wife took part in one of the most difficult rallies in the world - the Safari Rally. Mr. and Mrs. Zasada started on a Mitsubishi EVO rally car, and they took 12th place in the general classification of the rally, and 2nd place in their class. As it would later turn out, this was not the champion's last word either. Unfortunately, on 19 August 2021 his beloved wife Ewa, his multiple pilot whom he could always count on, died. Today, 92 year old Sobieslaw, who won 148 rallies during his long career, is the owner of several car dealerships.

Guinness World Record

On 24 June 2021, Sobieslaw Zasada took part in the Safari Rally, which is part of the World Rally Championship. The great champion was 91 years old at the time, thus becoming the oldest driver to take part in a WRC rally. He started on a specially prepared Ford Fiesta. Unfortunately, the champion did not finish this rally, he got stuck in a mud rut less than a kilometer before the finish line. This is just an unfortunate detail that cannot cover up this great feat. The champion said at the end that he hopes to beat this record at the age of 100!

A meeting after years

In September 2018, Patryk Mikiciuk, begins the restoration of a Porsche with an unusual Polish history. The car had been in Poland since the early 1970s. However, the project took a 180-degree turn when they learned the true history of this car, it turned out to be one of the rally cars of the champion Sobies≈Çaw Zasada. It was the famous Porsche 911 with registration number 77-07-KW. Since then Patryk and the ABcar team have been restoring the car to its former glory. The project culminated on December 6, 2021, when mister Sobies≈Çaw, who was 91 at the time, drove his car through the most famous rally section in Poland, the famous Criterium of Aces at Karowa Street. It was a great event for the whole Polish motor sport scene.
