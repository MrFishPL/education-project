Introduction


Rotmistrz Witold Pilecki- the hero from Auschwitz, we all heard his story at least once, on a history lesson, in radio or tv. His story, however, never found its time in our lives, so we invite you to get to know his inspirational person.

Youth


Born on May 13, 1901 in the Eastern Borderlands in a Polish family of the Leliwa coat of arms. He had 4 siblings, with whom he lived in Ołońc until 1910. He moved with his family to Vilnius, where he started studying at a trade school, and joined the scouting movement forbidden by the Russians. During World War I, after Vilnius was taken over by the Germans, he escaped with his family to his uncle in the in the area of Vitebsk. There he met with a conspiratorial, anti-occupation environment. In 1915, he was the deputy of the "Puhaczy", by the name of Prince Józef Poniatowski, but in 1916 he created his own scout team.

Conspiracy


When in 1918 Polish scouting came out of the underground, Pilecki and his troop attempted to rob a military warehouse, and then together with older scouts made their way to the reborn Poland. In the years 1918-1921 he served in the Polish Army, and fought during the war with the Bolsheviks. He spent the interwar period studying at the Stefan Batory University at the Faculty of Fine Arts, however he stopped his studies. Pilecki married Maria Ostrowska in 1931. In August 1939 he was mobilized to serve in the Polish Army. After the end of the September Campaign, Pilecki has made his way to Warsaw, where he has become one of the organizers of the underground organization of the Secret Polish Army, established on November 9, 1939, under the command of Major Jan Włodarkiewicz. Pilecki simultaneously performed two functions in this organization - that of the chief of the organizational and mobilization branch, as well as the chief of the armament department of the armament and special services division. He supervised and set up a network of secret hiding places for documents and firearms. The Auschwitz-Birkenau concentration camp caught the attention of the organization, to which Pilecki belonged, when the Gestapo arrested Władysław Surmacki - the Chief of Staff. During one of the TAP sessions, the matter of concentration camps was discussed. It was then proposed, that one of the members get through to him. It was declared, that the goal of this action would be to free the prisoners through an escape. According to some sources, Pilecki was to be the initiator, while according to others, the rotamaster was only to volunteer to carry it out.

Captivity in Auschwitz


Not much was known at the time the operation began about Auschwitz, it was only about 3 months after its inception. Pilecki was to infiltrate the camp and then obtain information about it, while helping the prisoners and keeping them spirited. On September 19, 1940, he was caught in a round-up in Żoliborz. In the camp, Pilecki founded the underground organization ZOW (Military Organization Union), which fulfilled the tasks set by the captain. Its operation was based on the Friday system, which was to spread the organization through groups of five leaders who did not know about each other, which ensured the organization's sustainability, regardless of the course of events. Pilecki prepared many reports in the camp, which he then sent to the command in Warsaw. For his underground activity, Pilecki, while still a camp prisoner, was promoted by General Stefan "Grot" Rowecki to the rank of lieutenant in November 1941. On the night of April 26-27, 1943, Pilecki and two fellow prisoners managed to escape from the camp. They got to the Vistula River by going along the railroad tracks, which they swam across by boat. After a long journey, the refugees managed to get to Nowy Wiśnicz, where Tomasz Serafiński put him in touch with the Polish Army units.

Curiosites


  • After the execution of Rotmistrz, his body disappeared. This disappearance was part of a campaign to forget the Polish hero by the communist authorities.To this day, it is not known where his body rests.
  • Witold Pilecki was sent to Auschwitz at his own request, where he organized the structures of the underground resistance, helping his fellow prisoners to escape.
  • Rotmistrz Pilecki had a son, thanks to whom the memory of Pilecki survived the times of particular communist ostracism.
  • Witold Pilecki was a Knight of the Cross of Valour, which he received in recognition of his heroism during the Battle of Warsaw.
  • During World War II, he became known as a good commander. Thanks to him, 7 tanks and 3 German aircraft were shot down.
  • He assumed the invented identity of a soldier, Tomasz Serafiński, and under this name organised the resistance movement in one of the concentration camps.
